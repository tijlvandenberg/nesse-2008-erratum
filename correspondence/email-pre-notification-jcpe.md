Dear Editorial Office,

I write as an informal pre-notification, not as a submission.

In the course of clinical implementation of the Periodontal Inflamed
Surface Area (PISA) framework introduced by Nesse et al. (2008)
[doi:10.1111/j.1600-051X.2008.01249.x], I have identified what appears
to be a single decimal-shift typographical error in Table 1, row
"maxillary lateral incisor", column a₅: the printed value 0.05890
appears to read 0.005890 in the worked example spreadsheets
the authors themselves provided as Figure 2a–c. The corrected value
reproduces all 25 anchored reference points across the three example
patients within 0.17 mm² (< 0.1 %), with full reproducibility code
available at the link below.

Today I have written to Dr. W. Nesse and his co-authors with the
underlying evidence and an invitation to consider issuing a Corrigendum
under their own authorship. **My strong preference is for the correction
to appear under the original authors' names alone, without me as a
co-author** — my motivation is service to the field, not authorship
credit. Should Dr. Nesse and colleagues decline or not respond within
approximately four weeks, I intend to submit a Letter to the Editor (or
Brief Communication, depending on your preferred format for this type
of correction) presenting the same evidence as a fallback.

I am sending this pre-notification so that, if the Office finds it
appropriate, the original article can be flagged in your internal
workflow and Crossmark service ahead of the formal Corrigendum
submission. The clinical impact is non-trivial — the printed coefficient
causes the polynomial to saturate the anatomical root surface at
clinical attachment levels around 4.6 mm, which is well within the
routine measurement range for moderate periodontitis at the lateral
incisors — and I would prefer to limit further uncritical propagation of
the printed value in citing software and cohort studies in the interim.

**No action is requested at this time.** The full materials, including
manuscript draft, figure, and a Python regression-test suite that
reproduces the example spreadsheets, are open at:

- Reproducibility (Zenodo DOI):  *[in te vullen na release]*

I will of course follow up via the official submission channel should
that become necessary. Please do not hesitate to contact me if any
clarification would be useful at this stage.
